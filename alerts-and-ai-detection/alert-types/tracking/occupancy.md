# Occupancy

Occupancy alerts you when the count of people, vehicles, or other objects in a defined zone rises above or drops below a threshold you set. It works for both overcrowding and under-occupancy scenarios.

## How it works

After drawing a zone in the camera frame, configure an occupancy threshold, such as "more than 5 people" or "fewer than 1 vehicle." Lumana counts the objects inside the zone and triggers the alert when the count crosses the threshold. You can set up separate alerts for overcrowding and for under-occupancy if both are relevant.

## When to use it

Occupancy monitoring is useful when the number of people or objects in a space directly affects safety or operations.

- Enforcing capacity limits in waiting rooms, lobbies, or queues.
- Detecting when a normally staffed area becomes empty during operating hours.
- Monitoring vehicle occupancy in parking zones or loading areas to prevent congestion.

Whether the concern is overcrowding or an unexpectedly empty space, occupancy alerting turns a count into an actionable signal. [Loitering](loitering.md) adds a time dimension to presence, triggering only when someone has stayed past the point where it becomes a concern.
