# Snapshot

Snapshot captures a still image from the camera view when triggered. Unlike video-based alerts, it saves a single frame rather than a clip. Use it when you need a lightweight record of an event without storing full footage.

## How it works

When the snapshot alert is triggered, Lumana captures a still image from the camera at that moment and stores it with a timestamp. You can configure it to fire on a schedule, in response to another alert, or based on defined conditions in the camera view.

## When to use it

Snapshot is useful when a still image is sufficient to document an event and full video storage would be excessive.

* Recording periodic visual check-ins of a location without continuous recording.
* Capturing a reference image at the moment a specific condition is detected.
* Reducing storage requirements in deployments where video evidence isn't always necessary.

In each of these cases, a well-timed still image provides the record you need without the overhead of continuous video storage.
