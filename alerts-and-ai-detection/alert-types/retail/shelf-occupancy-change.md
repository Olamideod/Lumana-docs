# Shelf occupancy change

Shelf occupancy change triggers when inventory on a monitored shelf changes faster than expected. Use it to detect sudden depletion, unusual restocking activity, or potential theft at the shelf level.

## How it works

Lumana monitors the occupancy level of a defined shelf area over time. When the level changes by more than a defined amount within a set window, the alert triggers and captures a clip of the shelf at the moment the change was detected.

## When to use it

This alert is useful when the rate of change matters as much as the current stock level.

- Detecting rapid shelf clearing that may indicate organized retail theft or sweep theft.
- Flagging unexpected restocking activity outside of scheduled replenishment windows.
- Monitoring high-value or high-shrinkage product areas where sudden stock changes are a risk indicator.

These scenarios all use rate of change as the signal, not just the current level.
