# Search video footage for other objects


## Introduction

In this article, we will explore how to efficiently search for objects directly from the camera player interface, eliminating the need to switch to the dedicated search function. This approach not only saves time but also streamlines the workflow, allowing users to access relevant information and video footage more intuitively and with fewer steps.

 

 

### Step 1:

Navigate to the specific camera you would like to scrub footage on

<div align="center"><img src="../.gitbook/assets/databases-analytics-and-search/search-other-objects-street-camera-view.png" alt="Lumana camera player showing a street-facing live view with playback controls and the timeline scrubber below." width="720"></div>

 

### Step 2: 

Scroll down to the Camera Metadata underneath the live view

 

<div align="center"><img src="../.gitbook/assets/databases-analytics-and-search/search-other-objects-camera-metadata-bar.png" alt="Camera metadata bar showing the time range picker, Thumbnails dropdown, and 24 Hours interval dropdown." width="600"></div>

 

### Step 3:
Select the dropdown next to the time and date labeled "thumbnails"

<div align="center"><img src="../.gitbook/assets/databases-analytics-and-search/search-other-objects-thumbnails-dropdown.png" alt="Thumbnails dropdown expanded showing options for Thumbnails, People, Faces, Vehicles, and License plates." width="240"></div>

 

### Step 4:   
Select whichever object you wish to scrub for, the options are People, Faces, Vehicles, and License Plates. 

 

 

### Step 5:
Once selected, all major objects will populate under the Camera Metadata section where you can quickly scrub through objects.

<div align="center"><img src="../.gitbook/assets/databases-analytics-and-search/search-other-objects-vehicles-results.png" alt="Camera metadata section filtered by Vehicles, showing thumbnail tiles of detected vehicles under a 'Yesterday' group." width="520"></div>

 

### Step 6:
If you want to conduct a deeper dive on a specific object, click on the object tile and it will allow you to view the video, object, and face or plate if applicable. You can also download the footage directly from this page.

<div align="center"><img src="../.gitbook/assets/databases-analytics-and-search/search-other-objects-object-preview.png" alt="Object preview panel showing Images, Video, Objects, and Faces tabs with HLS playback and a timeline scrubber for the selected camera moment." width="720"></div>

 

### Step 7:
You then have the ability to view the video, object, and face or plate if applicable. You can also download the footage directly from this page.


<div align="center"><img src="../.gitbook/assets/databases-analytics-and-search/search-other-objects-objects-tab-vehicle.png" alt="Preview panel with the Objects tab selected, displaying a cropped close-up of the detected vehicle." width="720"></div>

 

### Step 8:
Additionally you can archive footage directly from this page

<div align="center"><img src="../.gitbook/assets/databases-analytics-and-search/search-other-objects-create-archive-dialog.png" alt="Create archive dialog with Name, From and To time pickers, a duration slider, and a Create button." width="420"></div>
